CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

When CPR is Needed

CPR should be performed when a person is unresponsive and not breathing normally or not breathing at all.

CPR Steps for Adults

1

Check Responsiveness

Tap shoulders firmly and shout "Are you okay?"

2

Call for Help

Call 911 immediately or have someone else do it

3

Position Hands

Place heel of one hand on center of chest, between nipples. Place other hand on top, interlacing fingers

4

Chest Compressions

Push hard and fast at least 2 inches deep, 100-120 compressions per minute

5

Rescue Breaths

Tilt head back, lift chin, give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions

Choking Emergency

Signs of Choking

  • ‚Ä¢ Cannot speak, cough, or breathe
  • ‚Ä¢ Clutching throat with hands
  • ‚Ä¢ Skin turning blue or gray
  • ‚Ä¢ Loss of consciousness

Heimlich Maneuver Steps

1

Stand Behind Person

Position yourself behind the choking person

2

Make a Fist

Place fist above navel, below ribcage

3

Grasp and Thrust

Grasp fist with other hand, thrust upward and inward

4

Repeat

Continue until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious
